We reached Ujjain before sunrise after a long train journey.
The station was quiet, with only a few devotees heading towards the temple.
The Mahakal corridor stretches for kilometers, lined with lamps still glowing in the dark.
Thousands of people were already waiting in queue for the Bhasma Aarti.
As the priests began the ritual, smoke and chanting filled the air.
We ate fresh poha for breakfast near the ghats, watching the city wake up.
The Shipra river reflected the morning light as boats drifted slowly past.
Ujjain is one of those cities that feels ancient and alive at the same time.
